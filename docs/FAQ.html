General Questions

What is electronics recycling?

Electronics recycling (e-waste recycling) is the process of recovering, repurposing, or safely disposing of electronic equipment like computers, servers, networking hardware, and other IT assets. Responsible recycling keeps hazardous materials out of landfills and recovers valuable resources.

What is IT Asset Disposition (ITAD)?

ITAD is the practice of responsibly managing retired IT equipment to maximize value recovery while ensuring data security and environmental compliance. This can include resale, recycling, or documented destruction of IT assets.

Data Security

How do you handle data security?

All data-bearing devices undergo NIST 800-88 data destruction. We offer three methods:

  1. Software Wiping — Multi-pass overwrite with verification
  2. Degaussing — Electromagnetic destruction of all magnetic data
  3. Physical Shredding — Industrial shredding of hard drives and SSDs

A serialized Certificate of Destruction is issued for every device.

Can I witness the destruction of my equipment?

Yes. On-site data destruction is available where you can observe the process at your facility. Contact us to arrange.

What is a Certificate of Destruction?

A Certificate of Destruction is an official document that records:

  • Serial numbers of all destroyed devices
  • Method of destruction used
  • Date and time of destruction
  • Name of the technician who performed the destruction
  • EverTrade's signature and attestation

It's commonly referenced in HIPAA, SOX, and similar compliance audits. Read more about our data security practices →
